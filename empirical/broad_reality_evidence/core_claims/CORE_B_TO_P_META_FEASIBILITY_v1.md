# Core B-to-P meta-analysis feasibility v1

## Fixed role in the project

The research program already has one final biological hypothesis:

```text
Attraction A and floral defence/access restriction B are neither universally
substitutes nor universally complementary. Their relationship changes with the
antagonist-suppression benefit of B, the pollinator-use cost of B, and shared
costs between A and B.
```

The core B-to-P evidence layer has only one job:

> establish whether **some flower-specific chemical barriers can alter
> pollinator use**, and identify the conditions under which that cost is
> negative, absent, or reversed.

It does **not** prove a universal negative B-to-P coefficient, quantify a
universal `c_D`, establish B-to-H benefit, or empirically prove the
complementarity/substitution regime.

## The only primary quantitative question

```text
Among direct manipulations or standardized choice assays of flower/nectar
chemical B, within a stated natural-dose relation, how does higher B change
voluntary pollinator foraging preference, nectar consumption, or feeding rate?
```

### Core eligible outcome family

- paired or multi-option choice allocation;
- nectar volume removed, nectaring time, or feeding rate;
- count/proportion of voluntary use when the denominator and experimental unit
  are recoverable.

### Separate, non-poolable outcome families

- flower visitation rate;
- learning, memory, or flower constancy;
- pollen transfer;
- plant reproduction/fitness;
- microbial growth or pollinator health.

These outcomes may be mechanistically important, but they cannot be silently
converted into the same response as voluntary nectar use.

### Core trait scope

- nectar alkaloids, nicotine, cardenolides, or other explicitly deterrent/
  filtering secondary metabolites;
- experimental or choice-assay contrasts in chemical B;
- direct response of a legitimate pollinator.

Exclude from the core quantitative stratum:

- a floral volatile that is not experimentally assigned a defensive/filtering
  role;
- microbial manipulation when the chemical B contrast is not isolated;
- learning/memory without a voluntary-use outcome;
- nectar-thief or floral-antagonist response (these belong to B-to-H);
- treatments outside the reported natural range, unless analysed in a distinct
  supra-natural sensitivity stratum.

## What the current source set says

The prior source-resolution queue contains four direct B-to-P clusters, but no
full-text-adjudicated numerical contrast. Three of the four sit in the core
foraging/preference family, while *Delphinium barbeyi* is a visitation-rate
study and must remain separate.

| Status | Direct independent study clusters | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| strict core foraging/preference candidates | 3 | Gelsemium alkaloid/bumbebee, bird nicotine/feeding, Toxicoscordion zygacine/generalist-bee foraging |
| separate visitation candidate | 1 | Delphinium alkaloid/bumblebee activity and flower visits |
| full-text-adjudicated numerical core effects | 0 | no core meta-analysis may yet be run |

The Batch 1 abstract pass identifies two further **provisional** core candidates:

- *Nicotiana attenuata*: standardized choice tests; repellents reduce nectaring
  time and nectar removal but can increase visit number;
- *Gomphocarpus physocarpus*: natural-composition cardenolides reduce honeybee
  feeding rate while a wasp is the specialised pollinator.

They are candidates, not extracted effects. Both require source resolution,
dose adjudication, outcome definition, and panel-identity checks.

## Feasibility verdict at v1

```text
Current status: NOT READY FOR A PRIMARY POOLED META-ANALYSIS
```

Reason: the strict core has three candidate independent studies, and the
extended core has at most five plausible candidates before full-text source
review. No source-adjudicated numeric effect with variance has yet entered the
ledger. More candidate abstracts do not change this verdict.

The abstract record also already demonstrates why a one-number B-to-P average
would be biologically misleading:

- deterrence can depend on alternative floral resources;
- a compound can lower consumption but increase visitation or constancy;
- natural-range and supra-natural doses can reverse the apparent response;
- chemical filters can deter a generalist while retaining a specialist.

These are model-relevant conditions, not statistical nuisances to be averaged
away.

## Go / no-go gate

### Level 1: source resolution

Resolve the five strict-plus-provisional core candidates first. A source passes
only with a documented treatment/control or continuous contrast, natural-dose
relation, response unit, replicate structure, and uncertainty/raw data.

### Level 2: decide the analysis form

| Result after source resolution | Correct output |
|---|---|
| fewer than 3 numeric independent core studies | mechanistic case series; no pooled estimate |
| 3–4 numeric independent core studies | common-scale forest plot only when metrics are compatible; descriptive synthesis otherwise |
| at least 5 numeric independent core studies in one exact outcome/metric/design stratum | exploratory random-effects summary, explicitly condition-specific |
| at least 10 numeric independent core studies, with dose status and consumer/chemical identity retained | quantitative core synthesis; still no universal c_D claim |

A threshold alone never justifies pooling. Every effect must also share the
predeclared outcome, metric, intervention direction, and independent study
unit.

### Level 3: narrow additional retrieval, only if needed

Only after the five core sources have been resolved, targeted discovery may be
run for this exact missing stratum:

```text
(nectar OR floral nectar) AND
(alkaloid OR nicotine OR cardenolide OR deterrent secondary metabolite) AND
(pollinator OR bumblebee OR bee OR hummingbird OR wasp) AND
(choice OR preference OR consumption OR feeding OR nectaring)
```

This is not a new broad evidence map. It exists only to fill an explicitly
identified core numerical stratum, and it stops when two successive query
families yield no new eligible full-text effect or when all accessible candidate
sources have been screened.

## Parallel evidence layers

| Layer | Current proper role |
|---|---|
| chemical B-to-P core | restricted source-resolution pilot; may become a condition-specific meta-analysis |
| B-to-H floral defence | full census and mechanism-specific systematic synthesis; do not promise a pooled estimate yet |
| A-to-P | broad supporting map, not the central attraction-defence test |
| joint A/B/P/H studies | mechanistic examples, not an interaction meta-analysis |
| regime mathematics | establishes conditions for complementarity/substitution; not numerically fitted from separate route studies |

## Bottom line

The defensible empirical statement currently available is:

> Flower-specific chemical barriers can impose pollinator-use costs in some
> systems, but their sign and magnitude depend on dose, consumer identity,
> alternative resources, and the response layer being measured.

This premise is sufficient to motivate the conditional model. It is not enough
to claim that all floral defence reduces pollination, nor enough to call the
whole program a completed effect-size meta-analysis.
