# Four-path effect screen: *Rivea ornata*

## Source

Chitchak, N., Stewart, A. B. & Traiperm, P. (2024). *Trades-offs between
pollinator attraction and florivore defense maximize reproductive success in the
self-incompatible Rivea ornata (Convolvulaceae).* **BMC Ecology and Evolution**
24:115. DOI: `10.1186/s12862-024-02301-7`.

The article states that raw data are deposited in Mendeley Data, DOI
`10.17632/2n4vgpvzgs.1`.

## Screen question

Can this study contribute a recoverable direct effect to one of:

```text
A_flower → pollination
A_flower → floral antagonism
B_flower → floral antagonism
B_flower → pollination
```

## What is directly tested

| Experiment | Intervention / unit | Outcome | Four-path status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lepidopteran exclusion | physical exclusion of flying lepidopterans at flowers | fruit set | pollinator-access manipulation, **not** a floral A trait effect |
| Artificial/natural corolla damage | imposed or observed tissue removal categories | fruit and seed set | florivory-damage effect, **not** a floral B trait effect |
| Visitor observations | visitor group and time at 80 flowers over 530 observation hours | visit rate | visitor composition/rate, **not** a model of a measured A trait |
| Nectar measurements | nectar amount/concentration by time | no linked individual pollination model | descriptive trait measurement only |

## Why no registry row is entered

The paper contains strong information about pollination dependence, visitor
exposure, flower damage, and reproductive outcomes. It does not, in the article
text currently screened, estimate a direct effect of an independently measured
A trait on pollination or florivory.

The suggested protective mechanism—laticifers/latex within midpetaline bands—is
an anatomical explanation for selective florivore damage. It is not measured as
a continuously varying or experimentally altered plant-level B trait, and no
B→antagonism or B→pollination effect estimate is reported.

```text
A trait measured?                  yes, descriptive nectar/phenology/morphology
A trait → P or H estimated?        no
B trait independently quantified?  no
B trait → P or H estimated?        no
recoverable four-path effect?      no
```

## Value retained

This study remains useful for:

- documenting a pollinator-exclusion effect on fruit set;
- documenting experimental floral damage classes with reproductive outcomes;
- motivating future D2 designs that measure an A trait and an independent B
  trait on the same flowers/individuals; and
- testing whether the Mendeley raw package contains an unreported trait-level
  analysis worth a separate predeclared re-screen.

It is not used to manufacture an `A→P`, `A→H`, `B→H`, or `B→P` effect from
narrative functional interpretation.
