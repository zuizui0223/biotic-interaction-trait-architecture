## Why it stops at M1

The paper repeatedly frames flower traits as potentially relevant to both
pollinators and florivores, but its empirical behavioral response is the
florivorous-beetle channel. Its discussion explicitly leaves whether the observed
flower-size and chemistry differences attract pollinators for later work.

Therefore it cannot be upgraded through inference from a known pollinator
biology, literature citations, or the mere presence of pollen reward traits.

```text
Measured: floral traits → florivorous beetle response
Not measured: floral traits → pollination response
```

## Contribution retained

- Documents that attraction-associated floral traits and floral antagonism can
  be quantified together within a controlled chemotype design.
- Supplies candidate hypotheses for floral volatile/reward components, not a
  validated `B_flower` axis.
- Does not enter any direct D1 model or pooled pollination–antagonism analysis.
